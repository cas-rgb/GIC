# Social Ingestion Policy

Social media is supported, but not as blanket trusted data.

## Rule

- Platforms are carriers.
- Trust attaches to the specific account, not the platform itself.

## Supported platforms

- X
- Facebook
- YouTube
- Instagram
- TikTok
- LinkedIn
- WhatsApp Channels

These are supported because the official South African Government maintains accounts across them via the government social media directory:

- [Government social media accounts](https://www.gov.za/links/government-social-media-accounts)

## Whitelisting standard

Only ingest from accounts that are:

1. individually verified
2. tied to a real institution or trusted media brand
3. recorded in the social account whitelist
4. assigned a provenance and reliability tier

## Allowed account classes

- official government departments
- provincial governments
- municipalities and metros
- state utilities
- trusted media brands
- named journalists or civic monitors only if editorially governed

## Disallowed by default

- anonymous accounts
- repost aggregators
- parody accounts
- unverifiable community pages
- accounts previously flagged as fake or impersonation risk

## Why this matters

The Presidency has publicly warned about fake social media accounts impersonating public officials:

- [Presidency on fake social media accounts using Minister Khumbudzo Ntshavheni's name](https://www.gov.za/news/media-statements/presidency-fake-social-media-accounts-using-minister-khumbudzo-ntshavhenis)

So social evidence should be:

- secondary to official KPI truth
- auditable
- account-whitelisted
- clearly separated from deterministic analytics
